### Section: Legionella Risk at Golf Clubs

Golf clubs are a high-risk environment for Legionella. They combine hot tubs and spas (the single highest-risk recreational water feature for Legionella per the CDC), cooling towers for clubhouse HVAC, complex plumbing serving multiple locker rooms and kitchens, and a membership base that includes elderly and immunocompromised individuals who are most vulnerable to Legionnaires' disease.

Air pockets in water distribution pipes create stagnant dead zones where Legionella bacteria can colonize. Smart Valve™ reduces dissolved air and oxygen in the water supply, eliminating the conditions that create these stagnant zones. This is not a water treatment device — it does not add chemicals or replace a Water Management Program. It addresses one contributing factor: air entrainment.

ASHRAE Standard 188 requires Water Management Plans for facilities with cooling towers, hot tubs, and complex plumbing — all common at golf clubs. Smart Valve™ installation can be documented as a risk reduction measure within that plan. NSF 61 and 372 certified — safe for all potable water applications including pools, spas, and food service.
